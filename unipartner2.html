What to Pack for College

Essential Documents

  • ID and driver's license
  • Health insurance cards
  • University acceptance letter
  • Bank account information
  • Important medical records

Dorm Room Essentials

  • Bedding (sheets, pillows, comforter)
  • Towels and bathroom supplies
  • Storage solutions
  • Desk lamp and organizers
  • Laundry basket and supplies

Academic Supplies

  • Laptop and charger
  • Notebooks and pens
  • Textbooks (wait until syllabus confirms)
  • Backpack
  • Planner or calendar

Personal Items

  • Clothes for all seasons
  • Comfortable walking shoes
  • Toiletries and personal care items
  • Medications and first aid kit
  • Photos and items from home

First Week as a Freshman

Before Classes Start

  1. Complete any orientation activities
  2. Find your classes before the first day
  3. Set up your dorm room
  4. Meet your roommate and neighbors
  5. Locate important buildings (dining hall, library, health center)

Academic Preparation

  1. Purchase required textbooks
  2. Set up your student portal and email
  3. Introduce yourself to professors during office hours
  4. Buy a planner and note important dates
  5. Find study spots around campus

Social Tips

  1. Attend welcome events and activities
  2. Join clubs or organizations that interest you
  3. Keep your door open to meet people in your dorm
  4. Don't be afraid to introduce yourself to classmates
  5. Remember that everyone is looking to make friends too

Self-Care

  1. Establish a sleep routine
  2. Find healthy food options on campus
  3. Learn where the health center is located
  4. Take time to relax and adjust
  5. Call home if you're feeling homesick

How to Save Money in College

Budgeting Basics

  • Track your expenses for a month to understand spending patterns
  • Create a realistic budget that includes all expenses
  • Use budgeting apps to help manage your money
  • Set aside savings before spending on non-essentials
  • Review and adjust your budget monthly

Textbook Savings

  • Buy used textbooks or rent them
  • Check library reserves before purchasing
  • Look for international editions which are often cheaper
  • Share textbooks with classmates when possible
  • Sell books back at the end of the semester

Food Expenses

  • Meal prep to avoid expensive takeout
  • Take advantage of student discounts at local restaurants
  • Never go grocery shopping hungry
  • Use your meal plan effectively
  • Host potlucks with friends instead of eating out

Student Discounts

  • Always ask about student discounts (many places offer 10-15% off)
  • Use your student ID for discounts on software, streaming services, and more
  • Look for student priced events on campus
  • Take advantage of free campus activities
  • Use student travel discounts for breaks and weekends
